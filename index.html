The Odd-Even Methylene Spacer Effect

As reported in the paper, the supramolecular helicity of nanofibers assembled from naphthalimide-peptide building blocks is inverted with the alteration of methylene spacer units in the achiral segment. Even spacers (n=2, 4) tilt the local dipole transitions in an anticlockwise configuration, producing left-handed assemblies. Conversely, odd spacers (n=3, 5) enforce a clockwise twist angle, initiating right-handed helical nanofibrils.

DFT Ground-State Energetics

Density Functional Theory calculations at the range-separated hybrid functional level (ωB97X-D3BJ/def2-TZVP) confirm that noncovalent dispersion forces (E_disp) are predominantly responsible for this structural outcome. Dimer optimization maps indicate a global minimum at -40° and -50° twist angles for Even spacers, and +30° twist angles for Odd spacers, agreeing precisely with experimental Cotton effects seen in CD spectroscopy.
